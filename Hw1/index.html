DNA Computing

Home Timeline DNA:Hard Drive DNA:Computer


Every living thing is composed of DNA: Plants, animals, humans. Similar to the way computers carry out instructions in 0s and 1s, The genetic makeup of living organisms follow a set of instructions based off four components- Cytosine, Guanine, Adenine, Thymine. The order of these bases is what determine DNA's instruction set, or our genetic code- which allows us to develop, live and reproduce. In short, DNA computing is a branch of biomolecular computing concerned with the use of DNA as a carrier of info to make arithmetic and logic operations.
